SM Guaranty (ASC 460 / Rule 3A): The Sponsoring Member is unconditionally obligated to make FICC whole for all losses arising from a Sponsored Member default. FICC does not look to its Clearing Fund or surviving members unless the SM itself subsequently defaults.

⚠ Cascading Default — SM Also Defaults (After Covering HF)

If the Sponsoring Member cannot honour the Guaranty obligation (e.g., SM itself is in financial distress concurrent with a large HF default), FICC's own waterfall activates:

FICC Rule 4 GSD Waterfall (SM Default):
  1. SM's own margin deposits (net)
  2. SM's Clearing Fund contribution (~$100m example)
  3. FICC Retained Earnings (~$25m, "designated amount")
  4. Non-defaulting members' CF — pro-rata allocation
  5. CCLF — Capped Contingency Liquidity Facility draws
  6. Loss allocation / assessments (up to 2× CF per member)
Key Implication:
The SM's obligations to FICC (from the SM Guaranty) rank alongside its other FICC obligations. In a cascading default, FICC's mutualized resources are the backstop — not just for Sponsored Member activity, but for the SM's entire book of FICC-cleared trades. This is why SM Guaranty RWA = 100% EFC risk weight: the SM is fully exposed as principal guarantor.
